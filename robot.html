Robotics Teaching Assistant + Research Associate

Virginia Tech, Center for Design Research 2019-2020

During her Fall 2019 semester at Virginia Tech, Gabriella was the teaching assistant for the Introduction to Robotics in Architecture, Design, Art, and Construction course. This class was a web-based lecture with Dr. Nathan King, a professor working for Autodesk in Boston. Gabriella was present for students as a co-instructor to help them progress their research projects and teach them the basics of design robotics on three ABB IRB 120 industrial robot arms. Her tasks included running software tutorials in class, listening to research proposals, giving guidance, and training students to jog and run programs on the robots. She also assisted a student team on the large robot extruder designed for large format 3D printing.

In addition to teaching students, she researched large format additive manufacturing for her architectural thesis. She ran tests to determine possible geometries printed at this scale and defined ideal parameters for speed, temperature, and max overhang angle. Her research with large format additive continues on as she remains an employee at the Center for Design Research during her virtual semester graduate studies at Harvard's Graduate School of Design. Her current research focuses on non-layered printing through the exploration of form draping.
